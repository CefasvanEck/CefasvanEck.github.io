Survival Game:  The Structure world



Hello and welcome, my name is Céfas van Eck and this is my portfolio website where I show my biggest project ever: A Survival Game on a Hostile Island that somehow escaped the extinction of the dinosaurs.

Made in my own Game Engine called the MiystEngine, which started as a hobby 7 years ago, has led to a bunch of Level Design Tools that make the world feel more "alive".
Combined with my creativity, intrest in fossils and animals, has let to a long term project that I will keep working on, even after the release that is yet to be  announced.

The game simulates an ecosystem that is always changing and is filled in with Photoscanned and edited assets made mostly by me.




Holum Island is known for
its vulcanic activities

Heavily inspired by Peter Jackson's 2005 movie King Kong and the Book: World of Kong, this island is also known for its hostile environment, and I am not only talking about lava flows.

Dinosaurs and everything nature have always been important to me.
And with a Biology - Laboratorial diploma and many years of programming experience, I found it interesting to start experimenting with it in my WIP Game Engine in 2018.

Level Design

When I got Battlefield 4 in 2015, I started to get really interested when it came to Game Levels with many interactable objects and events.

Do you hop over a metal detector with a few seconds animation, or do you go through it and alert the enemy?

The island in the King Kong movie
always experienced seismic activity
but no Bassalt Pillars.
          So adding that would gibe my island something special!

I filled up many of the places with small and unusual creatures to make it feel like a real ecosystem.
These creatures don't have to be really detailed meshes with complex AI, but you need them to make the world not feel empty and barren.
AI mostly consists of walking, eating and fleeing from predators and the Player.

These are called Weta-Lobsters, a reference to the studio that designed the creatures for Peter Jackson's King Kong movie.

The texture came from Textures.com, a copyright free image website but I made some changes to the lobster!
