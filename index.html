INTRO

Hello and welcome, my name is Céfas van Eck and this is my portfolio website where I show my biggest project ever: A Survival Game on a Hostile Island that somehow escaped the extinction of the dinosaurs.
Made in my own Game Engine called the MiystEngine, which started as a hobby more than 8 years ago, has led to a bunch of Level Design Tools that make the world feel more "alive".
Combined with my creativity, intrest in fossils and animals, has let to a long term project that I will keep working on, even after the release that is yet to be announced.
The game simulates an ecosystem that is always changing and is filled in with Photoscanned and edited assets made by me.

When I recieved a copy of Battlefield 4 in 2015, I started to get really interested when it came to Game Levels with many interactable objects and events.
Do you hop over a metal detector with a few seconds animation, or do you go through it, letting the alarm go off and alert enemy Players of your presence?

Backstory of Holum Island


Heavily inspired by Peter Jackson's 2005 movie King Kong and the Book: World of Kong, my fictional island is also known for its hostile environment, and I am not only talking about lava activity.

Signs of tribe activity here

Dinosaurs and everything nature have always been important to me.
And with a Biology - Laboratorial diploma and many years of programming experience, I found it interesting to start experimenting with it in my WIP Game Engine in 2018. - Céfas van Eck, 20-06-2023

I filled up many of the places with small and unusual creatures to make it feel like a real ecosystem.
These creatures don't have to be really detailed meshes with complex AI, but you need them to make the world not feel empty and barren.
AI mostly consists of walking, eating and fleeing from predators and the Player.

These are called Weta-Lobsters, a reference to the studio that designed the creatures for Peter Jackson's King Kong movie.
The texture came from Textures.com, a copyright free image website but I made some changes to the lobster!

One of the Tools for my game, is something that feels like Minecraft's Creative mode but with realistic Assets.
You can place and rotate these Assets everywhere on the map!

Another Tool created by me is this Object Color Editing Tool. You can change the Contrast, Saturation and Brightness of each individual object.
Another thing you can edit is the transition from Terrain Texture to the objects Texture, making it blend in with the Terrain.
